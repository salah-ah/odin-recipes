A Traditional Menu for Hanukkah

Description

Hanukkah is a Jewish holiday that celebrates the rededication of the Second Temple in Jerusalem at the time of the Maccabean Revolt against the Seleucid Empire.

To celebrate this miracle, Jewish people eat a number of foods fried in oil, the most well-known of which are potato latkes and jelly donuts called sufganiyot.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1 egg
  • 1/2 cup vegetable oil
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup jam
  • 1/2 cup powdered sugar

Steps

  • Preheat oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C). Grease a 9x5 inch loaf pan.
  • In a large bowl, combine flour, baking powder, salt and sugar.
  • Make a well in the center and pour in egg, oil, milk and vanilla. Mix until smooth.
  • Pour batter into prepared pan.
  • Let cool in pan for 10 minutes, then turn out onto a wire rack.
  • When cool, spread jam over the top of the loaf.
